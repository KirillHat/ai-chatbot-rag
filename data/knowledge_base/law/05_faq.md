# FAQ

### Do you offer free consultations?
No. The 45-minute paid consultation ($250) buys you actual legal opinions
on your situation, not a sales pitch. If we're not the right fit, we'll
say so on the call and refer you elsewhere.

### How fast can you turn around an NDA review?
Standard mutual NDAs: 2 business days from receipt. Non-standard or
heavily marked-up versions: 3–5 business days. Same-day rush is
sometimes possible for existing clients, +50% on the fee.

### Can you operate on a contingency basis?
No. We are transactional lawyers, not litigators. We bill on fixed fees
or hourly with a cap.

### Are you admitted in my state?
Sarah is admitted in Massachusetts and New York. David is admitted in
Massachusetts and California. For matters in other states, we either
work with local counsel (we have a trusted network) or apply for pro hac
vice if it's a one-off.

### My company isn't incorporated yet. Can you still help?
Yes — that's actually one of our most common engagements. The Delaware
C-Corp founder package covers incorporation, bylaws, founder stock with
vesting, IP assignment and an initial board consent. $1,500 + state
filing fees.

### Can you help me sue someone?
Probably not. We don't take on litigation as a primary engagement. For a
demand letter or a pre-litigation negotiation, yes. If a lawsuit becomes
necessary, we refer to a litigator we know personally.

### Do you take equity instead of cash?
Occasionally, for early-stage clients we already work with on retainer,
and only on top of cash — never as a full substitute. If this is
something you'd like to discuss, raise it in the consultation.

### Is the AI assistant legal advice?
No. The chat assistant on this site can answer general questions about
our services, fees and process. It cannot give legal advice on your
specific situation. For that, book a consultation.

### Can the assistant share information about other clients?
No. The assistant only knows what's in our public knowledge base —
practice areas, fees, intake process and FAQs. It has no access to
client files, matter details or any privileged information.

### What happens to my data when I use the assistant?
The chat backend logs the conversation (anonymously, by random session
id) so we can improve the assistant. We never use chat content for
marketing, never sell it, and delete logs after 90 days. Don't paste
confidential or privileged information into the chat — call the office
instead.
